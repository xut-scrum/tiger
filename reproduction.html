Mating and Reproduction Information

Tigers can mate year round, with a stronger peak reported between November and April. Tigers are mostly solitary, so mating happens when territories overlap.

Key points

  • A tigress is in oestrus for a few days, with intervals of several weeks between cycles.
  • Gestation lasts around or over three months.
  • Litters can be up to seven cubs, but two or three is more typical.
  • Cubs stay with their mother for about two years, then leave to establish their own ranges.
