Anatomy and Biology

Tigers have a typical big cat body plan: strong forelimbs, wide paws, a large head, and a long tail. Their orange coat with black stripes is iconic, and stripe patterns are unique to each individual.

Body features

  • Front feet have five digits (including a dewclaw); back feet have four.
  • Claws are retractile and can reach about 10 cm in length.
  • Tigers have 30 teeth; their canines are among the longest in the cat family.

Size (varies a lot by population)

  • Head body length is roughly 1.4 to 2.8 m, plus a tail of about 0.6 to 1.1 m.
  • They stand roughly 0.8 to 1.1 m at the shoulder.
  • Siberian and Bengal tigers are among the largest populations described.
