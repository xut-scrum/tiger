Hello. You call me a tiger, but scientists use a special name too.

My scientific name

Panthera tigris

That is the official scientific name used today.

How I got my name

  • Step 1 (1758): Carl Linnaeus described the tiger and used the name Felis tigris.
  • Step 2 (1929): Reginald Innes Pocock studied tigers again and placed them in the genus Panthera, so the name became Panthera tigris.
  • Today: Scientists still use Panthera tigris.

The big cat club

Tigers belong to a cat family called Felidae. This family includes:

  • Lions
  • Leopards
  • Jaguars
  • House cats

Yes, your pet cat and a tiger are very distant cousins.

Are all tigers the same?

Not exactly. Tigers live in different habitats, for example:

  • Cold snowy forests
  • Warm jungles
  • Tall grasslands

Long ago, some scientists described 9 types (subspecies) of tigers. Today, some scientists group tigers into two big groups:

  • Mainland Asian tigers
  • Island tigers

On this website, we use simple names like:

  • Bengal tiger
  • Siberian tiger

Did you know?

  • Panthera is a group of big cats that can roar.
  • Each tiger has a unique stripe pattern, like a fingerprint.
  • House cats are in the same cat family (Felidae) as tigers.
