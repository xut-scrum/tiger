Behavior

Tigers live a mostly solitary life. Adults occupy home ranges and defend them from individuals of the same sex.

Territory and movement

  • A male home range can overlap with multiple females.
  • Tigers mark their ranges with scent signals such as urine spraying, scratching trees, and leaving other scent marks.
  • Young tigers disperse to establish their own ranges when independent.

Communication

  • Tigers use body language, scent, and vocalizations.
  • Roars are used to signal presence over long distances and can be heard for several kilometers.

People and conflict

  • Tigers usually avoid humans, but conflict can happen where people and tigers share the same landscape.
  • Conflict risk rises when natural prey is scarce and livestock becomes an easier target.
