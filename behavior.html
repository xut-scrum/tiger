How Tigers Behave

Tigers are usually quiet, careful animals. Most adult tigers live alone and travel around their own “home area”.

At a glance

  • Solo life: most adults live alone
  • Home area: a tiger uses the same region again and again
  • Messages: scent marks, scratches, sounds, body language
  • Safety: tigers usually avoid people

Home area and movement

  • A tiger’s home area can be very large, because it needs enough space to find food.
  • Male tigers often travel over bigger areas than females.
  • Sometimes a male’s area overlaps with areas used by several females.
  • Young tigers leave their mother when they are older and try to find their own home area.

How tigers communicate

Tigers do not “talk” like people, but they still send lots of messages.

  • Scent: they can leave smell marks (like a “note”) so other tigers know they were there.
  • Scratches: they scratch trees to leave signs and smells.
  • Body language: tail, ears, and posture can show feelings.
  • Sounds: they can growl and roar. A roar can travel far to let others know “I’m here”.

Tigers and people

  • Most of the time, tigers try to stay away from people.
  • Problems can happen when people and tigers live close together.
  • If wild prey is hard to find, a tiger may go closer to farms and livestock, which can cause conflict.

Did you know?

  • Tigers can swim well and may cross rivers to travel.
  • A tiger’s stripe pattern helps it blend into grass and shadows.
  • Tigers use smells and scratches like “signposts” in the forest.
